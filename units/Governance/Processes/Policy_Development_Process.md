# Policy Development Process

## Purpose

This process defines how organizational policies are developed, reviewed, approved, and maintained.

## Process Steps

### Step 1: Initiation

**Triggers for Policy Development:**

- New regulatory requirement
- Identified gap or risk
- Organizational change
- Strategic initiative
- Incident or audit finding

**Initiation Actions:**

1. Submit policy request to Governance
2. Assess need and scope
3. Assign policy owner
4. Establish development timeline

### Step 2: Drafting

**Policy Structure Requirements:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Purpose | Why the policy exists |
| Scope | Who and what it covers |
| Policy Statements | The actual requirements |
| Roles & Responsibilities | Who does what |
| Definitions | Key terms |
| Compliance | Enforcement and exceptions |
| Related Documents | Cross-references |
| Version History | Change tracking |

**Drafting Guidelines:**

- Use clear, unambiguous language
- Be specific about requirements
- Avoid jargon where possible
- Consider implementation burden

### Step 3: Review

**Review Stages:**

1. **Subject Matter Expert Review**: Technical accuracy
2. **Stakeholder Review**: Operational feasibility
3. **Legal/Compliance Review**: Legal adequacy
4. **Leadership Review**: Strategic alignment

### Step 4: Approval

| Policy Scope | Approving Authority |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Enterprise policies | Board or Executive Committee |
| Business unit policies | Business Unit Head |
| Functional policies | Function Head |

### Step 5: Publication & Communication

- Publish in policy repository
- Notify affected stakeholders
- Update training materials
- Brief managers on changes

### Step 6: Maintenance

**Review Cycle:**

- Annual review for all policies
- Regulatory trigger reviews
- Post-incident reviews

**Retirement Process:**

1. Assess continued relevance
2. Identify replacement if needed
3. Obtain retirement approval
4. Archive and communicate
