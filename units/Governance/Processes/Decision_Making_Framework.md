# Decision Making Framework

## Purpose

This framework establishes structured approaches for organizational decision-making to ensure consistency, transparency, and accountability.

## Decision Categories

| Category | Characteristics | Framework |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Strategic | Long-term impact, high stakes | Executive deliberation |
| Operational | Day-to-day, within guidelines | Authority matrix |
| Technical | Specialized expertise required | Expert consultation |
| Crisis | Time-sensitive, high impact | Escalation protocol |

## Decision Making Principles

### Clarity of Authority

- Decision rights clearly assigned
- Escalation paths defined
- No decisions by committee unless specified

### Informed Decisions

- Relevant data gathered
- Stakeholders consulted
- Alternatives considered

### Accountability

- Decision maker documented
- Rationale recorded
- Outcomes tracked

### Timeliness

- Decision timelines established
- Delays escalated
- "No decision" is a decision

## Decision Framework Models

### RACI for Decisions

| Role | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Responsible** | Makes the decision |
| **Accountable** | Owns the outcome |
| **Consulted** | Provides input before |
| **Informed** | Notified after |

### RAPID for Complex Decisions

| Role | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Recommend** | Proposes solution |
| **Agree** | Must approve |
| **Perform** | Executes decision |
| **Input** | Provides information |
| **Decide** | Final decision authority |

## Decision Process

1. **Define**: Clearly state the decision required
2. **Gather**: Collect relevant information and data
3. **Consult**: Engage appropriate stakeholders
4. **Decide**: Make decision within authority
5. **Document**: Record decision and rationale
6. **Communicate**: Inform affected parties
7. **Execute**: Implement decision
8. **Review**: Assess outcomes

## Escalation

| Trigger | Escalation Path |
|---------|-----------------|
| Beyond authority limit | Next level manager |
| Cross-functional conflict | Shared executive sponsor |
| Policy exception required | Policy owner |
| Legal/regulatory risk | General Counsel |
| Reputational risk | CEO/Board |
