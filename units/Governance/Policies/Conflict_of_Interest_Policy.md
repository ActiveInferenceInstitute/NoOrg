# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines conflicts of interest and establishes procedures for disclosure, evaluation, and management to ensure decisions are made in the best interest of the organization.

## Definition

A conflict of interest exists when an individual's personal interests, relationships, or activities could impair, or appear to impair, their objective judgment in fulfilling their responsibilities to the organization.

## Types of Conflicts

### Financial Interests

- Ownership or investment in vendors, customers, or competitors
- Outside employment or consulting with competing organizations
- Financial interest in transactions involving the organization

### Personal Relationships

- Family members employed by or doing business with the organization
- Close personal relationships that could influence decisions
- Nepotism in hiring or contracting decisions

### Fiduciary Conflicts

- Board members with interests adverse to the organization
- Executives with outside commitments affecting their duties
- Dual roles creating competing loyalties

## Disclosure Requirements

### Mandatory Disclosure

All covered individuals must disclose:

- Existing conflicts upon hire or appointment
- New conflicts within 30 days of arising
- Annual certification of conflict status

### Disclosure Process

1. Complete Conflict of Interest Disclosure Form
2. Submit to Compliance Officer or designated authority
3. Await assessment and determination
4. Follow prescribed management plan

## Management Approaches

| Conflict Level | Management Approach |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Low | Disclosure and monitoring |
| Medium | Recusal from specific decisions |
| High | Divestiture or role modification |
| Severe | Resignation from conflicting position |

## Review and Approval

- **Routine conflicts**: Reviewed by supervisor and HR
- **Material conflicts**: Reviewed by Compliance Officer
- **Executive conflicts**: Reviewed by Board committee
- **Board conflicts**: Reviewed by full Board (minus conflicted member)

## Consequences

Failure to disclose or manage conflicts may result in:

- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Contract termination for vendors/contractors
- Removal from Board or advisory positions
- Legal action if warranted
