# Records Management Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for the creation, maintenance, retention, and disposition of organizational records to meet legal, regulatory, and business needs.

## Scope

This policy applies to all records regardless of format including physical documents, electronic files, emails, and database records.

## Definitions

- **Record**: Information created, received, or maintained as evidence of business activities
- **Retention Period**: Minimum time a record must be kept
- **Disposition**: Final action taken on records (destruction, archive, transfer)

## Record Categories

| Category | Examples | Retention |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| Permanent | Corporate charter, Board minutes | Indefinite |
| Legal | Contracts, litigation files | 7+ years after expiry |
| Financial | Tax records, audits | 7 years |
| Employment | Personnel files | 7 years after termination |
| Operational | Correspondence, reports | 3-5 years |
| Transient | Drafts, convenience copies | Until superseded |

## Retention Schedule

- Maintained by Records Management
- Reviewed annually
- Legal hold supersedes schedule
- Disposition requires approval

## Record Management Lifecycle

### Creation

- Apply classification at creation
- Store in designated repository
- Apply metadata standards

### Maintenance

- Secure storage appropriate to classification
- Access controls enforced
- Version control where applicable

### Retention

- Follow retention schedule
- Apply legal holds when required
- Track retention expiration

### Disposition

- Verify no legal hold
- Obtain disposition approval
- Secure destruction for confidential records
- Document disposition

## Legal Holds

When litigation, investigation, or audit is reasonably anticipated:

1. Legal issues hold notice
2. Affected records identified
3. Normal disposition suspended
4. Hold released only by Legal

## Compliance

- Regular audits of records management
- Training for record custodians
- Consequences for non-compliance
