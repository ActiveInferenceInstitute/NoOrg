# Delegation of Authority Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the framework for delegating decision-making authority to ensure clear accountability, operational efficiency, and appropriate controls.

## Scope

This policy applies to all organizational decision-making including financial approvals, operational decisions, and legal commitments.

## Principles

### Clarity

- Authority limits clearly defined
- Documented and accessible
- No overlapping or conflicting authorities

### Accountability

- Delegations are personal and non-transferable
- Delegatee accountable for decisions made
- Regular review of exercise of authority

### Controls

- Appropriate segregation of duties
- Escalation paths defined
- Audit trail maintained

## Financial Approval Authority

| Level | Expense Approval | Contract Signing | Capital Expenditure |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|---------------------|
| Board | Unlimited | >$10M | >$5M |
| CEO | <$5M | <$10M | <$5M |
| CFO | <$1M | <$5M (financial) | <$2M |
| VP | <$250K | <$1M | <$500K |
| Director | <$100K | <$250K | <$100K |
| Manager | <$25K | <$50K | N/A |

## Operational Authority

| Decision Type | Authority |
|--------------|-----------|
| Hiring | Hiring manager + HR approval |
| Termination | HR + one-up manager |
| Promotions | Hiring manager + HR + budget approval |
| Policy exceptions | Policy owner |
| Vendor selection | Procurement + business owner |

## Legal Commitments

| Commitment Type | Signing Authority |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| NDAs (standard) | VP or above |
| Customer contracts | Per contract value matrix |
| Real estate leases | CFO or CEO |
| Employment offers | HR Director or above |

## Delegation Process

1. Define scope of delegation
2. Document in writing
3. Obtain appropriate approvals
4. Communicate to delegatee
5. Register with Governance

## Sub-Delegation

- Only permitted if original delegation allows
- Must be documented
- Original delegator remains accountable
- Not permitted for sensitive authorities
