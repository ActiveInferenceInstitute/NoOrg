# Whistleblower Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes a confidential and secure mechanism for reporting suspected misconduct, unethical behavior, or violations of law, policy, or regulations, while protecting reporters from retaliation.

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, officers, directors, vendors, and any other stakeholders who become aware of potential misconduct.

## Reporting Channels

### Internal Reporting

- **Direct Supervisor**: For routine operational concerns
- **Human Resources**: For workplace-related issues
- **Compliance Officer**: For regulatory or policy violations
- **General Counsel**: For legal matters

### Anonymous Reporting

- **Ethics Hotline**: Available 24/7, operated by independent third party
- **Online Portal**: Confidential web-based reporting system
- **Written Report**: Submit anonymously to designated P.O. Box

## Protected Activities

Employees are protected when reporting in good faith:

- Financial fraud or accounting irregularities
- Safety or environmental violations
- Harassment, discrimination, or hostile work environment
- Conflicts of interest or self-dealing
- Violations of laws, regulations, or company policies
- Retaliation against other whistleblowers

## Confidentiality

All reports are treated confidentially to the extent possible while allowing for proper investigation. Reporter identity is protected unless:

- Disclosure is required by law
- Reporter consents to disclosure
- Disclosure is necessary for investigation

## Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against good-faith reporters is strictly prohibited. Retaliation includes:

- Termination or demotion
- Harassment or intimidation
- Negative performance reviews based on reporting
- Exclusion from projects or opportunities

Any retaliation will result in disciplinary action up to termination.

## Investigation Process

1. **Receipt**: Report acknowledged within 48 hours
2. **Assessment**: Initial evaluation of allegations
3. **Investigation**: Thorough, impartial investigation
4. **Findings**: Documented conclusions and recommendations
5. **Resolution**: Appropriate corrective action taken
6. **Closure**: Reporter notified of resolution (to extent possible)
